Background

The project was developed to address critical issues faced by the homeless community, particularly resource wastage, uneven distribution, and the overemphasis on certain resources. These inefficiencies often led to an imbalance in how support was provided, leaving some individuals underserved while others received excess.

The inspiration came from collaborative research with HomeStreetHome, which highlighted these gaps in resource management and distribution. By working closely with NGOs, the project sought to align technological solutions with the real-world needs of homeless individuals, making the process of receiving and distributing resources more streamlined and impactful.

Design

The platform is divided into four sections: header, calendar, map, and footer, each serving a specific function in the resource distribution workflow.

HEADER

Welcomes users with a dynamic flip animation showcasing the project's title, slogan, and purpose, designed to make a strong first impression and convey the core mission of efficient and equitable resource distribution.

CALENDAR

Built with FullCalendar.js, the central feature displays registered resources by time, date, and location. Users hover over events to see resource type, quantity, location, and provider. A sidebar lists events by resource category so distributors can spot oversupplied or undersupplied items at a glance.

MAP

Displays distribution locations with both map and street views via a carousel. The top three gathering spots were chosen based on where the homeless community most frequently resides, minimizing confusion, overcrowding, and logistical issues at distribution points.

FOOTER & MOBILE

The footer provides contact details, social links, and donation opportunities for HomeStreetHome Taiwan. The entire platform was designed mobile-first, as many users rely on shared devices and public internet access.

Implementation

Independently led from start to finish, pitching the concept to NGOs, designing the system architecture, coding, testing, and final deployment.

  • Front-End: Vue.js for its flexibility, ease of integration, and reactive capabilities, ensuring a seamless responsive experience across all devices.
  • Back-End: Firebase for hosting and database management, chosen for its smooth integration with Vue.js and ability to scale alongside a growing user base without complex server management.

Future

Phase 2 will introduce two key features, currently in research and planning following the city government's expanded involvement:

  • Demand Registry: A form allowing the homeless community to list their most urgent needs directly, displayed in a sidebar so distributors can see real-time demand alongside available supply.
  • Location Pinning: An optional feature allowing the homeless community to pin their locations on the map, enabling medical and social services to provide targeted follow-up care while respecting privacy.

Closing

Eat Together has garnered attention from local governmental bodies including the Taichung City Government and the Social Affairs Bureau highlighting the platform's potential not only to address immediate challenges but also to shape public policy and urban management through optimized resource distribution.

As the platform moves into Phase 2, it remains flexible and scalable, poised to deepen its impact through enhanced communication between the homeless community and service providers.
