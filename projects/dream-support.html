Dream Support

A case management platform aimed at automating and streamlining social workers' workloads, empowering them to make data-driven decisions and focus on direct client support.

Background

The project was developed to alleviate a critical challenge faced by social workers: the overwhelming administrative workload that hindered their ability to provide direct care. The need to repeatedly input case data across platforms led to inefficiencies and delays in support.

Two core issues drove the project: fragmented social support programs with varying application requirements, and the reliance on manual, paper-based processes. This forced social workers to spend hours on repetitive tasks instead of focusing on their clients.

Dream Support centralizes both case and program management, automates key tasks, and introduces data-driven program recommendations, giving social workers back the time and focus they need for meaningful interventions.

Design

The platform is built around two modules, each targeting a different layer of the social worker's workflow.

CASE MANAGEMENT MODULE

Organizes client information across three tabs:

  • Case Profile Tab: Stores essential client information reused across applications, eliminating repetitive data entry.
  • Case Background Tab: Captures income, education, and other details that feed into the recommendation engine, which analyzes past cases to suggest programs most likely to succeed.
  • Application Record Tab: Tracks the full history of applications, including dates, programs applied to, and outcomes.

PROGRAM MANAGEMENT MODULE

Centralizes social support program creation and tracking in one place:

  • Program Creation: Social workers input program details and upload forms, keeping everything organized and accessible.
  • Pre-Filled Forms: Using stored client data, the platform auto-generates pre-filled forms for any registered program, dramatically reducing time spent on paperwork and freeing capacity for client-centered support.

Implementation

Independently led from start to finish, pitching the concept to NGOs, designing the system architecture, coding, testing, and final deployment.

  • Front-End: Vue.js for its reactive nature and component-based architecture, enabling a seamless interface and smooth future updates without disrupting existing functionality.
  • Back-End: Java for a stable, secure environment capable of handling a large number of concurrent users and complex case workflows with minimal downtime.
  • Database: PostgreSQL for efficient relational data storage, with scalability and complex query support well-suited to the growing volume of case and program data.

Future

Phase 2 will expand the platform's collaborative and informational capabilities:

  • Chatbot for Social Issues: A chatbot providing instant, field-specific responses to questions on poverty and social support, serving as a quick reference tool so social workers can better assist clients without lengthy research.
  • Support Ticketing Hub: A ticketing system allowing NGOs within the LearningFromThePoor alliance to raise cases requiring extra support, share resources, and collaborate on challenging situations. Tickets will also generate internal data for trend analysis and resource planning.

Closing

Dream Support has significantly eased the administrative burden on social workers, allowing them to dedicate more time to direct case support. By automating form generation and offering data-driven recommendations, the platform has transformed how NGOs manage cases, resulting in improved outcomes for clients.

Phase 2 will introduce the Support Ticketing Hub and chatbot, enhancing collaboration across the NGO network and providing instant access to tailored information. Dream Support is well-positioned to scale and continue driving meaningful improvements as needs across the network grow.
