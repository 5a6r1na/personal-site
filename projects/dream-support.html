Dream Support

A case management platform aimed at automating and streamlining social workers' workloads, empowering them to make data-driven decisions and focus on direct client support.

Background

The Dream Support project was developed to alleviate a critical challenge faced by social workers: the overwhelming administrative workload that hindered their ability to provide direct care. The need to repeatedly input case data across platforms led to inefficiencies and delays in providing support.

The project stemmed from two core issues: fragmented social support programs with varying application requirements and the reliance on manual, paper-based processes. This made it difficult for social workers to track programs and forced them to spend hours on repetitive tasks instead of focusing on their clients.

Dream Support streamlines these processes by centralizing both case and program management and automating tasks. With features like data-driven program recommendations, it also allows social workers to dedicate more focus to meaningful interventions. In its first phase, the platform aims to handle up to 1,000 active cases across 32 NGOs, with the potential to scale for long-term growth and greater impact.

Design

The Dream Support platform was designed with two primary goals in mind: ease of use and automation. Given the varied needs of social workers across multiple NGOs, it was essential to create an interface that was intuitive yet powerful enough to handle large volumes of data.

  • Case Management Module: This module organizes client information across three tabs: case profiles, background details, and application records, streamlining data entry and tracking progress. It also leverages the recommendation engine to suggest suitable programs based on case history.
  • Social Support Program Management Module: Social workers can create and manage programs in one place, generate pre-filled forms using stored client data, and keep programs updated. Form automation significantly reduces repetitive administrative tasks, allowing social workers to focus on client support.

The design of the Dream Support platform focuses on ease of use and automation, tailored to reduce the administrative burden on social workers. The platform is divided into two main modules: Case Management and Social Support Program Management, each serving a specific purpose in streamlining workflows.

  • Case Management Module: This module is split into three tabs:
    • Case Profile Tab: Social workers create and store essential client information, which is reused across applications, minimizing repetitive data entry.
    • Case Background Tab: Captures detailed background information, such as income and education, feeding into the platform’s recommendation system. The recommendation engine analyzes past cases to suggest programs most likely to succeed based on similar case histories.
    • Application Record Tab: Tracks the history of all applications, including dates, programs applied to, and outcomes, providing a clear view of each case's progress.
  • Social Support Program Management Module: This module allows social workers to create and track programs in a centralized place, simplifying program management:
    • Program Creation: Social workers can input program details and upload forms, ensuring all programs are organized and accessible for future applications.
    • Pre-Filled Forms: Using stored client data, the platform generates pre-filled forms for any registered program, saving time on paperwork. Automation of form generation reduces repetitive administrative tasks, freeing up time for more client-centered support.

Implementation

The project was implemented using a full-stack approach, centered around building a robust, scalable platform capable of handling up to 1,000 cases in its first phase across 32 NGOs. The development process was independently led from start to finish, including pitching the concept to NGOs, designing the system’s architecture, coding, testing, and final deployment.

  • Front-End: Vue.js was chosen for its reactive nature and ease of integration, providing a seamless, responsive user interface for social workers. Its component-based architecture also ensures future updates can be implemented smoothly without disrupting existing functionality.
  • Back-End: Java was used to ensure a stable and secure environment for managing the platform’s core logic. The robustness and reliability of Java allow the platform to handle a large number of concurrent users and complex case workflows with minimal downtime.
  • Database: PostgreSQL was implemented to handle relational data, ensuring efficient data storage and retrieval. Its scalability and support for complex queries make it well-suited for managing the growing volume of case and program data within the platform.

Future

Phase 2 of the Dream Support project will introduce two key features:

  • Chatbot for Social Issues: A chatbot will offer instant responses to questions on poverty, providing tailored, field-specific information to assist social workers.
  • Support Ticketing Hub: This system will allow NGOs to share information and request resources, fostering greater collaboration and resource distribution across the alliance.

These features aim to enhance the platform’s collaborative and informative functions, improving overall efficiency and support delivery.

As Dream Support evolves, we are planning several key features for Phase 2, aimed at expanding the platform’s functionality and fostering greater collaboration between social workers and NGOs.

  • Chatbot for Social Issues: A chatbot will provide instant responses to questions related to social issues, particularly in the realm of poverty. This tool will serve as a quick reference for social workers, offering them tailored, field-specific information to better assist their clients. While developing a comprehensive and accurate answer pool will take time, the chatbot is expected to significantly enhance the resources available to social workers by delivering useful, contextually relevant responses.
  • Support Ticketing Hub: A ticketing feature will be introduced to facilitate better collaboration among NGOs within the LearningFromThePoor alliance. Social workers will be able to raise tickets for cases requiring extra support, whether it's resource-related or handling a particularly challenging case. Other groups within the alliance can accept these tickets, promoting a cooperative environment where resources and expertise can be shared efficiently. The ticketing system will be flexible, allowing social workers to categorize and tag their requests. These tickets will also serve as valuable internal data for future analysis, helping NGOs understand trends in case needs and resource allocation.

These upcoming features will enhance Dream Support's role as both a management tool and a collaborative platform, ensuring that social workers have the tools and support they need to deliver the most impactful care.

Closing

Dream Support has significantly eased the administrative burden on social workers, allowing them to dedicate more time to direct case support. By automating form generation and offering data-driven recommendations, the platform has transformed how NGOs manage cases, resulting in improved outcomes for clients.

Looking into the future, Phase 2 will introduce the Support Ticketing Hub and chatbot, enhancing collaboration between NGOs and providing instant access to tailored information. With these features, Dream Support is well-positioned to scale and continue to drive meaningful improvements to meet growing needs across the NGO network.
